Micah currently works in the areas natural resource policy and management, political ecology and resilience. His research engages with the spatial dynamics of social and ecological resilience, resource tenure and power in the United States, Afghanistan and Southeast Asia.

Micah holds a BS in Agricultural Ecology from Wheaton College and a MS in Natural Resource Management. Since 2001, he has worked primarily in Bangladesh, the United Kingdom, Afghanistan and Lao PDR in the management of natural resources and environmental conservation. He is the son of a ninth-generation New England farmer and a father of three.
