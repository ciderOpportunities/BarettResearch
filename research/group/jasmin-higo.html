Jasmin is a first-year graduate student and Fulbright awardee from Munich, Germany. She is enrolled in the Master of Public Administration program with a concentration in International Development focusing on Development Economics.

Prior to Cornell, Jasmin handled several projects in different sectors. She worked for the technology company Siemens in Germany and Canada in the fields of sustainability and finance. Jasmin also worked for a Boston-based NGO on technology programs for low-income communities. In her free time, she initiated and led a development project in her country of origin Ethiopia.

At Cornell, Jasmin serves on the Graduate Student Advocacy Committee and works as a contributing writer for the Cornell Policy Review.
